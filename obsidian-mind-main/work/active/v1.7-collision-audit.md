# v1.7 Collision Audit ‚Äî Latent Issues in the Known Gotcha Families

> **Mode:** Findings + recommendations, no code changes. This is the systemic

> *next* latent issue in the same families already logged in [[brain/Gotchas]]:
> the swept-move raycast pattern, `Entity.intersects()`'s traversal-target trap,

**Method.** Full read of `collision_system.py`, `weapon.py`, `enemy.py`,

## F1. CONFIRMED ‚Äî bullets are raycast-visible to other bullets: shotgun pellet fratricide

`PlayerBullet` carries `collider='box'`; each bullet's swept ray ignores only
`[self, self.player]`; and a raycast hit kills the bullet **regardless of
`can_hit`** (damage is matrix-gated, dying is not ‚Äî `PlayerBullet.update`:
`if hit.hit: ‚Ä¶; self.die()`). The shotgun spawns 5 pellets at one point, so
they immediately hit each other: measured **5 spawned ‚Üí 1 alive two frames
later, in an open field**. The shotgun has shipped at ~1/5 effectiveness since
v1.5. Pistol/rifle escape only because sequential shots are ~7.5u apart.

**Note the asymmetry:** `EnemyBullet` has **no collider** ‚Äî enemy bullets are
invisible to every ray. The player-bullet collider serves no positive purpose
found in this audit (nothing needs to raycast-hit a player bullet).

**Recommendations (either closes F1; (a) also closes F2):**
(a) drop `collider='box'` from `PlayerBullet`, matching `EnemyBullet`; or
(b) extend bullet-ray ignores with both bullet layers via
`collision_manager.query_layer(...)`, exactly as `swept_move_blocked` already
does for character movement. If the collider is kept for some future purpose,
(b) is mandatory anyway.

## F2. LATENT ‚Äî the player-bullet collider leaks into two more ray consumers

Because player bullets are collidable, they are candidate hits for every ray
that doesn't ignore them:
- **Player ground/ceiling rays**: `vertical_ignore = [self] + triggers +
  pickups` ‚Äî a bullet crossing under the player's feet can read as "ground"
  for a frame (false landing / early gravity cut).
- **Enemy LOS ray** (`_is_occluded`, `ignore=[self]`): a bullet crossing the
  enemy‚Üíplayer sightline reads as occlusion ‚Äî flickers the health-bar
  visibility and can make `DetectPlayerNode` lose the target for a tick.
Triggering condition: busy firefights, bullets crossing sightlines ‚Äî i.e.
normal play, at low probability per frame. Fix falls out of F1(a) for free.

## F3. CONFIRMED ‚Äî bullet spawn point skips thin cover at point-blank

Bullets spawn at `gun.world_position + camera.forward` (~1.3u ahead of the
eye). Standing flush against the armory's 0.5u wall and firing into it, the
bullet spawned at x = ‚àí9.32 ‚Äî **past the wall's far face (‚àí8.0)** ‚Äî and flew
on. Any wall ‚â≤1u thick is shootable-through at zero range; the swept ray only
protects the path *from the spawn point onward*.
**Recommendation:** trace the segment eye‚Üíspawn-point before the first flight
frame (one extra raycast at fire time), or spawn at the camera and offset only
visually. Same treatment conceptually as the viewmodel's dual-camera split:
visual muzzle ‚âÝ ballistic origin.

## F4. LATENT ‚Äî EnemyBullet's fixed 1u ray: tunneling on frame hitches + 1u early hits

`PlayerBullet` derives its ray length from actual travel
(`speed*dt + 0.2`); `EnemyBullet` uses a **fixed `distance=1`** while moving
`speed*dt` per frame. Two consequences:
- a frame hitch where `10 * dt > 1` (dt > 100ms ‚Äî window drag, GC, disk I/O)
  lets an enemy bullet step through a thin wall or through the player;
- at normal dt the ray *over*-reaches: the bullet "hits" (and deals damage)
  up to ~0.8u before visually arriving ‚Äî a small but real reach extension on
  every enemy shot.
**Recommendation:** mirror PlayerBullet's `speed*dt + skin` ray length.

## F5. CONFIRMED ‚Äî ground contact doesn't converge: buried at spawn, floating after landings

Measured: rest **y = 0.5 at spawn** (feet embedded to the ground plane's
centre; ground speed 0.4 u/s vs tuned 8.0 because the feet-level swept ray
starts inside the ground and blocks everything ‚Äî one jump un-sticks it,
permanently), and rest **y = 1.594 after a jump** (feet ~0.59 *above* the
floor, indefinitely). Mechanism: `grounded` is any down-ray hit within
origin‚àí0.1 ‚Üí 1.0u, gravity zeroes while grounded, and the snap corrects only
*upward* (`if player_bottom < ground_top`). So standing height is "wherever
you arrived", anywhere in a ~1.1u band. The burial half is seeded by
`FirstPersonController.__init__`'s own ground snap (origin-to-surface, not
feet-to-surface).
**Recommendation:** make the grounded branch converge both ways
(`player_bottom = ground_top` exactly, or ease toward it), and set the spawn
y so feet start on the surface. Flag for a hands-on feel pass afterward ‚Äî
eye-height consistency changes how everything else reads
([[v1.7-playtest-findings]] ¬ß2.2‚Äì2.3, ¬ß5).

## F6. LATENT (plausible, one consistent observation) ‚Äî no fall-step clamp: high-speed falls can tunnel

`self.y += vertical_speed * dt` is unclamped; fall speed after a 5u drop is
~21 u/s; the ground probe looks down only 1u from origin‚àí0.1. A single
50‚Äì100ms frame (dt spike) at that speed steps 1‚Äì2u ‚Äî through the 1u-thick
ground or any thin platform. Probe 3's post-wall-jump excursion (a frame
showing pure sky through the backface-culled ground, then a kill-plane
recovery) is consistent with exactly this, with the screenshot disk-write as
the dt spike ‚Äî but it was observed once and not isolated, so this stays
**plausible**, not confirmed. The giant y=‚àí25 kill plane currently doubles as
the safety net.
**Recommendation:** clamp per-frame vertical displacement (e.g.
`min(v*dt, 0.8)` against the 1u probe), or lengthen the down-ray to cover
`|v|*dt + margin`. Cheap; removes a whole failure class independent of
whether the probe-3 event was this.

## F7. CONFIRMED ‚Äî two jump systems drive `y` simultaneously

`Player.input('space')` sets `vertical_speed = 18` **and** then calls
`super().input(key)` ‚Üí `FirstPersonController.jump()` ‚Üí `animate_y(...)` (an
Ursina tween that writes `y` directly, collision-blind, with FPC's own
`jump_height`/curve). Both write `y` every frame; measured effective apex
**1.8u** vs the physics design's 3.6u ‚Äî the tuned `jump_force = 18` /
`gravity = 45` are largely dead knobs, and the tween portion ignores ceilings
while active. Also `FPC.jump()` flips `self.grounded` on its own schedule.
**Recommendation:** stop forwarding `'space'` to `super().input` (keep the
custom physics jump as the single authority), then re-tune apex by hand ‚Äî
whatever apex Ivan liked was a property of the *mixed* system, so the parkour
tower must be re-checked after (see the playtest report's tower notes).

## F8. CONFIRMED ‚Äî Enemy.shoot's 1.0s self-gate clamps every faster preset

`Enemy.shoot()` keeps its own `can_shoot` cooldown at
`ENEMY_SHOOT_COOLDOWN = 1.0` regardless of the preset's `AttackNode` cooldown.
`aggressive` (0.4s) and `patrol_then_attack` (0.8s) are silently clamped to
1.0s ‚Äî measured: aggressive fired 2 shots in 3s. `AttackNode`'s docstring
states the lockstep assumption ("with matching durations") that the fast
presets violate.
**Recommendation:** either drop the entity-level gate (the node owns cadence;
`Cooldown`-decorated presets already prove node-owned timing works), or set
`enemy.shoot_cooldown` from the preset. Decide once, document in
[[brain/Key Decisions]] ‚Äî this is the "two sources of truth for fire rate"
twin of F9.

## F9. CONFIRMED ‚Äî two sources of truth for detection: `look_at` tracks through walls

`Enemy.update()` rotates toward the player whenever
`distance ‚â§ self.detection_range` ‚Äî the **entity attribute (100 for every
enemy)**, not the preset's `DetectPlayerNode` range, and with no LOS check.
Measured on an occluded patroller: body facing = bearing-to-player (244¬∞)
while walking heading 273¬∞. Enemies crab-walk patrols staring through walls;
undermines the v1.7 detection-gate work visually. Same split brain as F8:
node-level truth (range + LOS) vs entity-level truth (bare distance).
**Recommendation:** gate the `look_at` on the tree's detection outcome (e.g.
let `DetectPlayerNode` set `enemy.player_detected` and read that), or at
minimum reuse `can_see_player()` on the same throttle as `_occluded`.

## F10. Verified-OK (worth recording so it isn't re-audited)

- **`intersects()` discipline**: `TriggerZone.update` and `AmmoPickup.update`
  both use the corrected no-arg + membership pattern from the Gotcha ‚Äî no
  other `intersects(entity)` misuse exists in `Scripts/`.
- **Ghost/preview collider rule**: the browser drag ghost is built with
  `collider=None` (`editor_browser.py:994`) per the Gotcha; `model_preview`
  likewise has no collider. (The ghost's *positioning math* has a separate,
  non-collision bug ‚Äî [[v1.7-editor-ux-audit]] ¬ßD5.)
- **Diagonal corner seam**: probed the armory's outer corner with a diagonal
  swept walk ‚Äî clean block-and-slide, no seam penetration (an earlier
  probe-1 reading that suggested penetration was a measurement artifact;
  re-tested deliberately in probe 2).
- **Layer matrix**: `PICKUP`/`TRIGGER` correctly absent from
  `COLLISION_MATRIX`; both correctly ignored by `swept_move_blocked` AND the
  player's vertical rays; `can_hit` asymmetry intact; pool release paths
  symmetric with `collision_manager` add/remove. Menu-return teardown left
  the scene at a stable 72 entities across three cycles ‚Äî no tracked-set
  leaks.
- **`main_menu()` nuclear sweep + `_is_live` guards**: no new unguarded
  `e.name` reads found in the current teardown paths.

## F11. Minor observations (no action urged)

- `COLLISION_MATRIX`'s `WALL` bit is decorative ‚Äî walls are never registered,
  so the bit never matches; harmless but misleading to a reader.
- `CollisionManager.query_near` has **zero callers** (and its 3√ó3-cell
  neighborhood silently assumes `radius ‚â§ cell_size=5` if anyone ever does
  call it ‚Äî worth a docstring line the day it gains a user).
- The player's ground ray passes a 2-tuple direction `(0, -1)` where every
  other site passes a `Vec3` ‚Äî works, but one style.
- Enemy hitbox (3√ó3√ó3 box) vs drone visual (~1.1u wide): shots that visibly
  miss the mesh still land. Deliberate (collider predates the model swap,
  kept intentionally ‚Äî [[brain/Gotchas]] .glb entry); recording it here as
  the likely explanation if hands-on play ever reports "hit registration
  feels generous".
- `MuzzleFlash` sets `always_on_top=True` then immediately re-enables depth
  test/write ‚Äî the two fight; cosmetic-only, worth one look whenever the
  muzzle flash is next touched.
